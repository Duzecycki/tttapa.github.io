Detailed Description

template<uint8_t K, class int_t>
class EMA< K, int_t >

A class for single-pole infinite impulse response filters or exponential moving average filters.

Straightforward implementation of the difference equation with a slight optimization by using a factor of two as the pole location (this means that no division or floating point operations are required).

Difference equation: \( y[n] = \alpha·x[n]+(1-\alpha)·y[n-1] \) where \( \alpha = \left(\frac{1}{2}\right)^{K} \), \( x \) is the input sequence, and \( y \) is the output sequence.

An in-depth explanation of the EMA filter

Fixed-point integer arithmetic with a precision of \( 2K \) is used.

Template Parameters
KThe amount of bits to shift by. This determines the location of the pole in the EMA transfer function, and therefore the cut-off frequency.
int_tThe (signed) integer type to use for the input, intermediate values and the output. Should be at least \( M+1+2K \) bits wide, where \( M \) is the maximum number of bits of the input. In case of the Arduino's built-in ADC, \( M = 10 = \log_2(1024) \).
